Whitewater Rafting Company

A Whitewater Rafting company organizes trips, provides the watercraft, life jackets, etc., and typically offers experienced guides to take passengers down a river. The business intent is to provide a thrilling and safe experience to individuals and groups for a fee.

History

Commercial rafting history evolved from early scientific expeditions in the 19th century to the first commercial trips in the 1940s, with the industry taking off in the 1960s and 1970s as companies formed and became popular, especially in

the western United States. Key developments include the use of surplus military rafts after World War II and the rise of specialized companies offering guided trips on iconic rivers like the Grand Canyon, eventually leading to the establishment of organizations like the International Federation of Rafting (IRF) in 1997.
